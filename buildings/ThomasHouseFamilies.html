Thomas House Families

The home, located at 503 Brown Street, was home to three prominent Niles industrialists- James Ward, John Thomas and Jacob Waddell. While the home is not one of the largest homes in the city, its history is what makes it stand out among other homes in the city.

Located on the east bank of the Mosquito Creek, south of the Erie RR, it was originally the William Ward and Company, built in 1870.

During that period of time, the Ward family lived in a house which was located at the corner of Park Avenue and Main Street, which later became a commercial hotel and in 1918 became the location of the Security Dollar Bank. Today (2023) Farmer’s National Bank occupies the site.

Rear view of the Ward-Thomas Museum.

Built in 1870 by William Ward and known as the Wm. Ward & Co blast Furnace, it failed in the Panic of 1873.

Mrs. Thomas grew most of the flowers herself in the greenhouse which was located just a few yards from the main house.
