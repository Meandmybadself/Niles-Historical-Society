Niles McKinley High School.

As the veterans of World War 2 returned home, the large number of babies born in this time period became known as 'The Baby Boom'. In anticipation of the expansion of schools that would be needed, a bond issue was passed in 1953 to build two new elementary schools, Lincoln Elementary and S.J. Bonham, and a new high school, Niles McKinley High School. In addition, the old Niles High School would be renamed Edison Junior High School, serving all 7-9 grades in Niles. One old elementary school, Roosevelt, which was near to the new high school would become the high school annex in a few years.

McKinley High School. S11.121

Niles McKinley High School, 616 Dragon Drive, was built in 1956 and dedicated in October of 1957.

The main high school was designed for 750 students upon its opening.

The gymnasium, named War Memorial Gym, had a seating capacity of over 2000.
